# Core Modern Floristry Research Pack Design

Date: 2026-05-27

## Purpose

Create a structured research pack for filling future Flowers Cards. The pack should support the product direction: a practical floristry workbook disguised as a beautiful visual atlas.

The document must help transform flower knowledge into cards that are useful, sourced, and professionally structured.

## Scope

Use approach 2: the core of modern floristry.

The first research pack covers around 35 key flowers and materials used in contemporary floristry. It is not a complete encyclopedia of every possible botanical material.

Other flowers should be listed as structured queue entries for later filling, but without pretending they are complete.

## Per-Flower Information Structure

Each flower entry should include:

- Russian name;
- English name;
- Latin name;
- practical seasonality;
- role in composition;
- visual character and emotional effect;
- freshness checks at purchase;
- processing after purchase;
- storage and vase life;
- common beginner mistakes;
- three popular varieties, types, or market groups;
- "all other varieties" link;
- card-ready copy;
- source notes.

## Variety Rule

The research pack should not pretend that three varieties cover all diversity.

For flowers with thousands of cultivars or many market groups, use this model:

1. three useful market groups or categories for beginner floristry;
2. optional examples of named varieties inside each group;
3. a separate "all other varieties" link to Wikipedia, Wikimedia, RHS, breeder catalogs, or another reliable index.

For flowers with narrower floristry usage, the three entries can be actual varieties, forms, or color/shape groups.

## Source Rules

Use sources by layer:

- botanical identity: botanical references, extension resources, RHS, Kew, Missouri Botanical Garden, NC State Extension, Wikipedia only as orientation where appropriate;
- florist handling: professional florist care guides, grower/supplier care sheets, postharvest references;
- varieties: breeder catalogs, grower pages, RHS, Wikimedia/Wikipedia lists where available;
- visual/card inspiration: not used as factual authority.

Every completed flower entry should have source notes with direct URLs.

## Output Files

Create:

- `research/core-modern-floristry-flowers.md`
  - main research pack;
  - first five flowers fully filled;
  - remaining flowers listed in structured queue.

Update or create:

- `research/source-log.md`
  - source log for flower research;
  - include topic, source, URL, type, trust level, and use.

## Success Criteria

The research pack succeeds if:

- it can directly feed future card writing;
- first five flowers have practical florist-level detail;
- variety/type blocks are honest and expandable;
- source links are traceable;
- incomplete flowers are clearly marked as queue items, not finished entries;
- the structure can scale to the remaining flower list.

## Out Of Scope

- rewriting the HTML card UI;
- collecting final images;
- claiming complete cultivar coverage;
- building a full encyclopedia in one pass;
- unsourced factual claims presented as final.
