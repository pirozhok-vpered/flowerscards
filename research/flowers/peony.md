# Peony

**Names:** Пион / Peony / *Paeonia* spp.; common cut-flower peonies are often herbaceous peony cultivars, especially *Paeonia lactiflora* and hybrids.

**Seasonality:** Strongly seasonal. Local field peonies peak late spring to early summer. Import and stored peonies can extend availability, but the flower still reads as a seasonal luxury. In card logic: "spring-early summer hero."

**Composition Role:** lush focal, soft mass, romantic seasonal statement, luxury wedding/editorial flower. Works best when allowed space to open; not ideal for tight, hard-edged designs where every bloom must stay the same size.

**Character:** Soft abundance, transition, celebration, garden luxury. Peony changes dramatically from small round bud to huge open flower, so it teaches timing better than almost any spring flower.

**Freshness Checks:**

- Bud should show color and feel like a soft marshmallow for reliable opening; rock-hard green buds may fail to open.
- Avoid fully blown flowers if the bouquet must travel or last several days.
- Check for sticky residue, bruised petals, botrytis, or blackened outer petals.
- Stems should be firm and leaves healthy; limp leaves can indicate dehydration.
- Buy by event timing: tight colored buds for later, softer/opening stems for immediate visual impact.

**Processing After Purchase:**

- Remove lower foliage to reduce water loss and keep water clean.
- Recut stems cleanly at an angle.
- Hydrate in deep clean water in a cool place.
- Allow time for opening; do not force by placing in hot/sunny conditions unless the job requires accelerated bloom and risk is acceptable.
- If storing, keep cool and monitor stage daily; peonies can open quickly once warm.

**Storage And Vase Life:** Often 5-10 days depending on harvest stage and room temperature. The "marshmallow stage" is key: it gives both opening reliability and better vase performance. Cool storage slows opening; warmth accelerates it. Once fully open, the peak decorative stage is shorter.

**Beginner Mistakes:**

- Buying hard green buds and expecting them to open for tomorrow's event.
- Buying fully open blooms too early for a wedding/install.
- Leaving too much foliage, causing water loss and crowding.
- Forgetting that one stem can double or triple visually as it opens.

**Three Useful Varieties / Types / Groups:**

1. Herbaceous peony — the main cut-flower workhorse; dies back in winter; many classic double and semi-double forms.
2. Tree peony — woody stems, huge dramatic blooms, more garden/collector logic than everyday cut-flower base.
3. Itoh / intersectional peony — hybrid between herbaceous and tree peonies; often strong stems and unusual colors; useful as a premium knowledge layer.

**All Other Varieties:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peony — broad species/cultivar overview. For named cultivar work, cross-check with American Peony Society or grower catalogs.

**Card Labels:** late spring-early summer / focal / timing-sensitive / high type complexity

**Card-Ready Notes:**

- poster card: "Пион — цветок ожидания: маленький бутон превращается в большое садовое облако."
- story card: "Главный урок пиона — стадия. Он красив не только цветком, но и моментом раскрытия."
- care card: "Buy at colored soft-bud stage, strip foliage, hydrate cool, plan opening time."
- varieties card: "Begin with herbaceous, tree, and Itoh groups before going into named cultivars."
