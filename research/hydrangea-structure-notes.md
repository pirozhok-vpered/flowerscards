# Hydrangea Structure Notes

Date: 2026-05-24

## Why Varieties Need Their Own Layer

For beginner florist cards, "hydrangea" is too broad. The practical card should distinguish:

1. Botanical group or market type.
2. Flower/head shape.
3. Fresh vs antique/dried stage.
4. Cut-flower handling.
5. Garden/pruning facts only when they explain market seasonality or type differences.

## Main Groups To Track

- Hydrangea macrophylla: bigleaf hydrangea; common florist/cut-flower reference group; mophead and lacecap forms.
- Hydrangea paniculata: panicle hydrangea; cone-shaped inflorescences; often different visual role from round mopheads.
- Hydrangea arborescens: smooth/tree-like hydrangea in Russian casual speech; includes Annabelle-type round white/green heads.
- Hydrangea quercifolia: oakleaf hydrangea; cone-shaped clusters and distinctive leaves; less like the classic florist mophead.
- Hydrangea serrata: mountain hydrangea; related visually to lacecap forms.

## Card Structure Recommendation

Use three levels:

1. Species card: broad behavior and handling for "Гортензия".
2. Type strip: macrophylla / paniculata / arborescens / quercifolia / serrata.
3. Variety notes: cultivar names, color, head shape, source region, and handling caveats.

This prevents the care card from becoming an encyclopedia while still making it more professional than a generic beginner note.

## Cut-Flower Handling Notes To Verify Per Source

- Hydrangea is hydration-sensitive.
- Typical vase life is often around 5-10 days, depending on stage, cultivar, storage, and hydration.
- Fresh-stage and antique-stage hydrangeas can behave differently.
- Recutting and hydration treatment matter.
- Some florist sources recommend full-head soaking or boiling-water/alum recovery methods for wilted stems; these should be labeled as florist techniques and verified carefully.

## Sources To Use In Full Research Pass

- RHS pruning guide for group differences.
- NC State Extension Plant Toolbox for species descriptions.
- OSU Extension for general hydrangea care and cut bloom handling.
- Peer-reviewed postharvest hydrangea vase-life papers for cut-flower behavior.
- Florist supplier care sheets only as practical market notes, not as botanical authority.
